Grace Hopper was a groundbreaking computer scientist and U.S. Navy rear admiral who helped pioneer programming in the early days of computing. She developed the first compiler and played a major role in creating COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages. Edith Clarke

Edith Clarke was a trailblazing electrical engineer and the first woman to earn an electrical engineering degree from MIT. She made major contributions to power system analysis and invented the Clarke Calculator, which simplified complex electrical calculations.
