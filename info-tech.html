Code Galaxy Online Coding Classes

Live, online coding classes for Rochester students covering Scratch, Python, web development, and more. Flexible scheduling and small class sizes.

Tech Academy – Video Game Design

Rochester Community Education offers summer camps for grades 3–7 where students create games using Construct 3 while learning programming fundamentals.

YEL! Coding Animation and Video Games

Hands-on Scratch programming for students in grades 3–6. Offered through Rochester Community Education with a focus on creativity and exploration.

Winona State University – Computer Science

Undergraduate majors available at the Rochester campus including Computer Science and Applied Computer Science. Offers hybrid and evening classes.

Explore WSU

Rochester Community and Technical College

Associate degrees in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, and Bioinformatics. Programs are aligned with Winona State University for transfer options.

P-TECH 535 Information Technology Pathway

Earn a high school diploma and associate degree in IT through this free program. Includes college courses, mentorships, and internships from IBM and Mayo Clinic.

Learn About P-TECH

Rochester Public Library Code Club

Code Club offers students in grades 4–12 the chance to learn basic programming through fun projects. Led by library staff and volunteers, sessions are free and held at the Rochester Public Library.

Code Club Info

Girls Who Code (Rochester Area)

Free programs for 3rd–12th grade girls to learn computer science and join a supportive community. Clubs meet after school at various locations in and around Rochester.

Girls Who Code

Technovation Girls (Minnesota Chapter)

A global tech education nonprofit offering girls the chance to learn app development and entrepreneurship. Minnesota students participate in teams, guided by mentors to solve real-world problems with technology.

Technovation Website Technovation Minnesota

BDPA (Black Data Processing Associates) Minnesota Chapter

Provides technology education and networking for youth and professionals of color. Offers High School Coding Competition (HSCC), mentoring, and STEM workshops. Rochester students may connect through Twin Cities chapter events.

BDPA Twin Cities National BDPA

Destination Imagination – Southeast MN

Encourages creativity and tech-based problem-solving through challenges that often include app design and computational thinking. Programs are available to K–12 students.
